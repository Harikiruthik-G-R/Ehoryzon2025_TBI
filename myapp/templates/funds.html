Spark Fund Guidelines

     Proposal Raising

Once the spark fund is granted, mentors need to raise proposal and can receive funds by submitting an advance money form at the KEC office (with all supporting documents).
30-50% amount of sanctioned amount can only be raised as advance money.
Mentor is entirely responsible for finance-related matters.
It's recommended to start purchasing after the Proposal is raised for the project.

     Bill Settlement and Component Registration

Bills must be dated after the project sanctioned/proposal raised date (Date after the principal signs).
Bills have to be raised in the name of principal, Kongu Engineering College, Perundurai, Erode.
GST invoice (with Date, Sign, and seal) is preferred.
If any bill without GST is claimed, it needs proper clarification and committee approval, (Only for lathwork or any similar nonstandard works.)
Bill settlements can be done only after committee approval (After the demo and millstones are achieved).
A copy of all documents has to be maintained.
Partial bill settlement may be permitted as per committee approval and work satisfaction.
Before final bill settlement, the mentor needs to provide the utilization certificate (refer to Section III – UC and Final Report) and evidence for milestone achievements such as patents, copy rights, awards or anything similar.
Before final bill settlement, all the purchased items need to be recorded in the research/project cubicle lab register book and signed, similar to the lab purchase procedure.

     Utilization Certificate and Final Report

After submission of the Utilization Certificate (UC), only final bill settlement is permitted.
Mentors need to provide UC in the given format.
Font: Times New Roman, Font Size: Title: 18 pt, Headings: 14 pt, Para: 12 pt, spacing: 1, Tables and figures must be named properly.
Paper Size: A4, Border: Yes
Footer: Spark Fund, Page Number, Project ID.
1st Page: Project Title, Project Duration and Details, Details of Students Involved, Budget Utilization, Milestones Achieved
2nd Page: Problem Statement, Objective, Proposed Method
3rd Page: Block Diagram of Executed Work, Solutions, Fund Utilization and Details, Output Photos (Minimum 3 to 4 photos)
Last Page: Outcomes with Evidence

     Change in Team Members / Mentor

If mentors need to change student innovators or alter the team in any given case, such as students who have gone on internships, placements, graduated, or student work that is not satisfactory, they can do so after notifying to the committee.
If there is a need to change mentors, the committee has the right to take a decision.
If any above team must submit the form given below. (Refer: Spark Fund – Member/Mentor Change Form)

     Other Important Conditions

Projects must be in TRL 3 levels (Refer below figure 1.) and final milestone should be a patent/product/copy right/ technology transfer, if not application will be rejected.
If funds are not properly utilized / milestones are not met / very slow or no work progress is made / or any other issues arise, the committee has all the rights to cancel funding at any time.
Mentor and students must present all the review monthly, without fail and must follow the committee's insights.
Patents or copy rights need to be filled out with respect to the KEC procedure for IPR filing.
If a patent or copyright has to be transferred to the person involved in the work or any other firm, or if you want to tech-transfer or any other form of similar work, KEC norms must be followed.
All the conditions and norms may be changed without notice and adapted as per KEC's new norms and regulations. (Now & this when new norms are implemented)

Technology Readiness Level (TRL) Scale

What is TRL? Technology Readiness Level (TRL) is a method for estimating the maturity of technologies during the acquisition phase of a program.

Important Note

Projects must be at least at TRL 3 level to be eligible for Spark Fund. The final milestone should be a patent, product, copyright, or technology transfer.

Need Assistance?

For any queries regarding Spark Fund Guidelines or application process, please contact the KEC office.
